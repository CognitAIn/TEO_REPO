### Abstract

The TEO (Thermal-Energy Optimization) and EcoCode framework introduce a new class of computing architecture based on *homeostatic balance* — a self-regulating state where thermal, computational, and electrical demands remain in harmonic equilibrium.  
This discovery redefines optimization not as peak performance but as sustained stability across variable workloads and physical substrates.

---

### Scientific Basis

EcoCode operates on the principle that every digital process produces measurable thermodynamic and electromagnetic strain.  
By integrating sensors, feedback loops, and resonance-based control algorithms, the framework aligns computational energy flow with biological equilibrium models.  
It implements *Δ + 1 harmonic balancing* to damp oscillations and prevent overshoot, ensuring predictable performance and power stability across time scales.

Mathematically, the governing objective function is:  
\J = Σ_t [ L(t) + λ·P(t) + β·Var(L_t) ]\  
where *L* is latency, *P* is power draw, and *Var(L)* is temporal variance.  
Adaptive control minimizes *J* by modulating process allocation and frequency in real time.

### Engineering Paradigm Shift

Traditional optimizers chase local maxima in throughput or efficiency.  
EcoCode replaces that paradigm with **homeostatic computing** — continuous adaptation toward a target equilibrium.  
Every node acts as an autonomous regulatory unit that self-adjusts under feedback, while global coherence emerges through shared telemetry.  
The result is hardware-agnostic stability that scales from embedded devices to supercomputing clusters without external orchestration.
